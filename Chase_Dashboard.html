▸ The Two Blacksmiths (tap to read)
Two apprentices were each given a block of iron and told to forge a blade by sundown.

The first worked for six hours. He'd heat the iron, then wander off. Check his phone. Come back, strike the anvil a few times. Heat it again. Walk away. By sundown, he had a dull, warped piece of metal.

The second worked for ninety minutes. He heated the iron until it glowed white, then struck with precision — every blow placed exactly where it needed to go. No wasted motion. No wandering. By lunch, he had a blade that could split a hair.

The master said: "The fire doesn't care how long you stand near it. It only cares whether you strike while the iron is hot."

Your brain works the same way. Fifty focused minutes builds more neural pathways than four distracted hours. The science proves it. This isn't about time — it's about intent per minute.
50 FOCUSED MINUTES beats four distracted hours. Every time.
